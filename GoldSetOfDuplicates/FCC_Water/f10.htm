Family demands FCC pull radio station linked to water death

Last Update: 1/22/2007 7:25:25 PM

Save/Share Story
diggreddit
newsvineGoogle
del.icio.usYahoo!


SACRAMENTO (AP) - Lawyers for the family of a Rancho Cordova woman who died after drinking nearly two gallons of water in an on-air radio contest are demanding the Federal Communications Commission terminate the station's license.

The development comes as the Sacramento County Sheriff's Department is investigating the death of 28-year-old Jennifer Lea Strange. Also lawyers representing Strange's family are threatening to file a wrongful-death suit against the station.

The attorneys say personnel at radio station K-D-N-D should have realized that Strange was in poor condition when she left the station following the contest. Her body as found at her home hours later.

The Sacramento area radio station fired 10 employees after Strange died January 12th. She was one of about 18 contestants who tried to win a Nintendo Wii gaming console by seeing how much water they could drink without going to the bathroom.
