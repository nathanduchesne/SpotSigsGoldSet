Gov. Crist Signs Insurance Reform Bill

POSTED: 10:34 am EST January 25, 2007
UPDATED: 10:43 am EST January 25, 2007
Email This Story | Print This Story
Sign Up for Breaking News Alerts
TALLAHASSEE, Fla. -- Gov. Charlie Crist signed a bill on Thursday that he and others hope will lower Floridians' property insurance costs.

The measure was passed by lawmakers in a special session on Monday. It was the first bill signed by Crist, who took office earlier this month.

The governor's message to Floridians was "Help is on the way." The bill aims to cut into the skyrocketing cost of home insurance that many Florida residents have seen since the busy hurricane seasons of 2004 and 2005. Many have seen premiums more than double or triple.
