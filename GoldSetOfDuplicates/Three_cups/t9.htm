    Moderate caffeine in pregnancy
    2007-1-27 
    Change font size:
    -- Advertisement --

    DRINKING moderate amounts of caffeine during pregnancy does not lead to premature births or underweight babies, Danish scientists said yesterday.

    Up to three cups of coffee a day does not seem to have a negative impact on the baby or the pregnancy.

    Earlier studies that looked at the impact of moderate caffeine consumption during pregnancy have produced mixed results. Some showed no difference while others suggested too much caffeine could lower average birth weight by 100-200 grams.

    "In our study we found no effect from caffeine," said Dr Bodil Hammer Bech, Institute of Public Health, Aarhus, Denmark.

    Unlike other research projects in which women who had given birth were asked how much coffee they drank while pregnant, the Danish scientists monitored 1,207 pregnant coffee lovers who were randomly selected to drink either a caffeinated or decaffeinated brew in the second half of the pregnancy.

    The women did not know which group they were in.

    "We had two groups and we actually found no difference between the average birth weight for moderate intake of caffeine - about three cups," said Bech, whose findings are reported online by the British Medical Journal.

    "The difference in the weight of the babies between the two groups was 20 grams," she said.
