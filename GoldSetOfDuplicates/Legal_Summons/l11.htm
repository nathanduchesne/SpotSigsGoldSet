Fox Seeks YouTube User’s Identity [The Wall Street Journal]

January 26th, 2007

Fox subpoenas video sites over pirated episodes of “24” and “The Simpsons” to reveal uploading users’ identities.

The Wall Street Journal is reporting that Twentieth Century Fox has subpoenaed video sites YouTube and LiveDigital to learn the identity of the user that uploaded four at-the-time unaired episodes of the hit television show “24″, along with twelve reruns of the long-running animated series “The Simpsons”.  The four hourlong episodes of “24″ actually appeared prior to their primetime January 14th premier; Fox has stated in its subpoena that it became aware of the pirated episodes on January 8th, and notified YouTube at that time.

YouTube’s subpoena, which was filed on January 18th in the U.S. District Court for the Northern District of California, seeks the real identity of a user by the name “ECOtotal”, and reads, in part: “Fox has not authorized this distribution or display of the [episodes].  The subpoena request YouTube, Inc. to disclose information sufficient to identify the Subscriber so that Fox can stop this infringing activity.”

The same episodes were uploaded by user “Jorge Romero” to lesser-known video site LiveDigital, to which Fox also apparently issued a subpoena.  The Wall Street Journal is reporting that a spokesman for LiveDigital stated that the material was taken down immediately.
