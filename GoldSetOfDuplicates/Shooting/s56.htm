Student arrested in Washington high school shooting

Last Updated: Wednesday, January 3, 2007 | 6:37 PM ET

CBC News

A 17-year-old boy was shot and killed in a Tacoma, Wash., high school hallway as classes began on Wednesday morning and a fellow student was arrested about two hours later in a nearby suburban area, police said.

The circumstances that led to the shooting are under investigation but students speculated it was race related.

Witnesses said three shots were fired at around 7:30 a.m. at Foss High School.

Freshman Sam Sao, 14, said she was in the lunchroom, waiting for the bell to ring, when the shots were heard.

"Everyone was yelling, 'Get in the gym! Get in the gym!"' she said. "At first we thought it was a fight. Then the teachers started getting on the tables and screaming."

The victim has been identified as Samnang Kok, 17. No one else was injured, police spokesman Mark Fulghum said.

The teenaged suspect, whose name was not released, was caught about two hours later in a suburban area dotted with homes and shopping centres.

The International Baccalaureate school, which draws some 1,700 students to its programs, was cordoned off with yellow tape and locked down as police continued to patrol the property.

Students were evacuated from the school and officials cancelled classes Wednesday, the first day back after the holiday break.

Aerial video footage of the school showed staff doing head counts and students calmly descending stairs and being loaded onto buses. The building's U.S. flag was flying at half-mast in honour of the passing of former U.S. president Gerald Ford.
