NBA legend Michael Jordan, wife, file for divorce

Updated Fri. Dec. 29 2006 5:44 PM ET

Associated Press

CHICAGO -- Michael Jordan and his wife Juanita filed for divorce after 17 years of marriage.

"Michael and Juanita Jordan mutually and amicably decided to end their 17-year marriage," the couple said in a statement Friday issued through their lawyers. "A judgment for dissolution of their marriage was entered today. There will be no further statements."

Juanita Jordan previously filed for divorce in January 2002, but withdrew her petition a month later when the couple announced they were attempting a reconciliation.

During her last divorce petition, Jordan said attempts to reconcile their marriage had failed and future ones "would be impractical and not in the best interests of the family."

The couple met at a Chicago restaurant during his second season with the Bulls and were married Sept. 2, 1989, in Las Vegas. They have three children: Jeffrey, Marcus and Jasmine.

Jordan, 43, a part-owner of the Charlotte Bobcats, led the Chicago Bulls to six NBA titles and was a five-time league MVP. He also played with the Washington Wizards and served as the club's president of basketball operations for a brief time.

Jordan lives in Highland Park, Ill.
