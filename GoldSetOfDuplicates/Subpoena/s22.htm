Last Update: Saturday, January 27, 2007. 0:00am (AEDT)

Fox subpoenas YouTube over illegal TV uploads

Twentieth Century Fox has subpoenaed Google Inc.'s YouTube video service to learn who uploaded pirated copies of episodes of television shows.

The Wall Street Journal reports the offending episodes were from the hit shows 24 and The Simpsons.

The subpoena asks YouTube to hand over information to identify the subscriber so Fox can stop the infringement.

The four-episode season premiere of thriller show 24, starring Kiefer Sutherland, appeared on the site ahead of its television broadcast and 12 episodes of the Simpsons were being distributed on YouTube by a subscriber called "ECOtotal".

Fox said it officially notified YouTube about the episodes and requested immediate removal or to disable access to the service.

The same group of episodes were uploaded under the username of Jorge Romero on the video site LiveDigital, to which Fox also issued a subpoena.

A spokesman for LiveDigital told the Journal the material was taken down right away.

Google and Fox officials were not immediately available for comment.

- Reuters
