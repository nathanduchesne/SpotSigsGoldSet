Woman, 65, saves husband by fending off mountain lion

Associated Press
SAN FRANCISCO -- Wildlife officials Thursday credited a woman with saving her husband's life by clubbing a mountain lion that attacked him while the couple were hiking in a California park.
Jim and Nell Hamm, who will celebrate 50 years of marriage next month, were hiking in Prairie Creek Redwoods State Park when the lion pounced.
"He didn't scream. It was a different, horrible plea for help, and I turned around, and by then the cat had wrestled Jim to the ground," Nell Hamm said in the hospital where her husband, 70, was recovering from a torn scalp and puncture wounds.
After the attack, game wardens closed the park 320 miles north of San Francisco, and later shot and killed a pair of lions found near the attack site.
"Jim was talking to me all through this, and he said, 'I've got a pen in my pocket . . . Jab him in the eye,"' said Nell, 65. The pen "didn't want to go in as easy as I thought it would."
When the pen bent and became useless, Nell Hamm went back to using the log. The lion eventually let go.
The couple then walked a quarter-mile for help.
