Youth arrested after pupil is shot dead

A STUDENT was shot dead in the hallway of a US high school as classes were starting yesterday morning.

Following the attack police arrested another student found wandering a few miles from Foss High School in Tacoma, Washington.

Many details of the 7:30am shooting, including the murdered student's age, were not immediately released, but police said no-one else was hurt.

The police spokesman, Mark Fulghum, said police were trying to determine what prompted the attack.

The suspected killer, another Foss student, was captured about two hours later without putting up a fight, detective Chris Taylor said.

The shooting happened on the first day back after the winter break. Sam Sao, 14, said she was in the lunchroom when the shots were fired.

"Everyone was yelling, 'Get in the gym! Get in the gym!"' she said. "At first we thought it was a fight. Then the teachers started getting on the tables and screaming."

Student Jacki Phongsavath said: "I thought it was fireworks. I looked around the corner and saw someone laying on the ground and blood on the lockers."
