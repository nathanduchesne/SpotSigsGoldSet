Co-Pilot of Comair Plane Said It Was 'Weird' There Were No Runway Lights

Jan 17, 2007 09:13 AM

WASHINGTON - Just seconds before the plane crashed in Kentucky, the co-pilot of a Comair flight remarked that it was "weird" that there were no lights on the runway.

The final seconds of that Aug. 27 flight were revealed in transcripts made public Wednesday by federal safety investigators. The plane was trying to take off on the wrong runway in Lexington, Kentucky. The runway had no lights and was too short for a passenger jet.

After the co-pilot's comment, the captain responded "yeah" and soon after, said "whoa" and then the sound of impact is heard, as flight 5191 ended with the deaths of everyone but the co-pilot. 

Forty-nine people died in the wreckage of America's deadliest plane crash in five years. The co-pilot lost a leg, suffered brain damage, and says he does not remember a thing about what happened.
