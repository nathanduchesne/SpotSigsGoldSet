Murdoch's Fox hunts YouTube uploader of "24" shows, MySpace plans to double footprint and Norway gives iTunes until March to open

By Mark Sweney / Digital Digest 11:39am

News Corporation-owned 20th Century Fox has subpoenaed YouTube to find out the identity of a user who uploaded episodes of "24" before their primetime premiere.

The subpoena, which was uncovered on the blog Google Watch, was granted by a judge in U.S. District Court in San Francisco after being filed on the 18th January.

The user of the Google-owned Youtube video-sharing website uploaded four episodes of "24" in early January before its official premiere on Fox on the 14th of January.

In addition, 12 episodes of the Simpsons season seven were also uploaded.
Filed on the basis of the Digital Millennium Copyright Act, 20th Century Fox served YouTube with the subpoena yesterday.

Fox has been unable to identify the user and wants help to avoid "irreparable harm" to its business. The company apparently became aware of the clips on YouTube on the 8th of January.

Norway gives Apple until March to agree to 'unlocking' iTunes platform

Regulators in Norway have deemed Apple's iTunes store illegal because it breaks consumer protection laws because its songs only work on iPods.

The news came to light yesterday reported yesterday by Infoworld stating that the government ombudsman in Norway has given Apple until 1 March to say if it will agree to change its restrictive Fairplay digital rights management policy.

Then Apple has until 1 October to open the system to other MP3 Players or further legal action could ensue, which could involve a shutdown of the website if necessary.

Earlier this week, the Norwegians aligned with German, French and Finnish consumer groups to strengthen pressure on Apple to loosen its restrictive DRM business model for all of Europe.

MySpace to double overseas expansion this year

The expansion would mean that MySpace would look to be in 11 markets by the end of the year.

It is currently primarily in Western markets - such as the US, UK, Ireland and Australia - and Travis Katz, senior VP for international operations at MySpace, admitted in an interview that cracking Asia was high on the agenda, but also posed the biggest challenge to its current approach.

It has tested services in Mexico, in addition to Italy and Spain

Mobile instant messaging photoblog coming to a phone near you

Mobile instant messaging company Colibria is launching a new mobile photo blogging service.

The product, it says, will provide "another leap forward" for the world of citizen journalism by allowing users to take photos on their camera phones and instantly upload them to an online portal where they can be viewed.

Hearst aims for web 2.0 communities around magazines to be running by April

Hearst Digital Network, the online division of the publisher behind titles including Cosmopolitan and Esquire, is aiming to build a platform to allow all its titles to enable users to build "groups" as they do in the likes of Yahoo! or MySpace.

Hearst Digital, operating within NatMags in the UK, said last year it was aiming to do this.
