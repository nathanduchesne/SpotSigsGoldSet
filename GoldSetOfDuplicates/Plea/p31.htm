Prince’s plea to paparazzi: stop harassing my girlfriend
STEWART PATERSON January 10 2007

Prince William has said he wants "more than anything" for paparazzi to stop pursuing Kate Middleton, his girlfriend.
Yesterday as Ms Middleton celebrated her 25th birthday, amid increasing rumours that the couple will announce their engagement, more than 20 photographers and several television crews waited outside her home in Chelsea, west London.
Both the prince and lawyers acting for Ms Middleton have appealed for her to be left in peace.
The Press Complaints Commission has been notified of discussions with newspapers, but as yet no formal complaint has been made.
A London newspaper group has said it will not use agency photos of Ms Middleton.
News International, whose titles include the Sun and News of the World, did not say it will not print pictures of Ms Middleton, but the agency ban means it will only be able to print photographs taken by its own staff.
As Ms Middleton left home for her job as a fashion buyer, she was again confronted by a waiting media presence.
Paparazzi on motorbikes were pursuing the Princess of Wales when she was killed in a car crash in Paris in 1997 along with Dodi Fayed.
A spokesman for Prince William said: "We are pleased that News International has agreed to stop using paparazzi pictures.
"What Prince William wants more than anything is for the paparazzi to stop harassing her."
Solicitors Harbottle and Lewis, who also acts for the Prince of Wales, is understood to be involved in negotiations with newspapers and magazine publishers seeking to protect Ms Middleton's privacy.
However, her legal representatives are reported to be preparing a legal case to prevent intrusion of privacy, in case a persuasive approach should fail.
In 2005, the law firm contacted publishers complaining of harassment, stating she was being followed daily.
Last week, 10 police officers were called in to protect the couple as they left a London nightclub where a herd of photographers waited outside. A police officer was on duty outside her home yesterday.
The prince's statement comes as a preliminary hearing began for the inquest into the death of Diana, Princess of Wales.
The scenes outside Ms Middleton's home are reminiscent of the press attention received by Prince William's mother in the months before her engagement was announced to the Prince of Wales.
Photographers camped outside her flat in 1980 to take her picture as she left for work at a London nursery.
Diana's Mother, Frances Shand Kydd, complained about press intrusion before the couple's engagement was officially announced.
The media agreed to allow Prince William to study at St Andrews University without unsolicited publicity.
During his time there he met Ms Middleton and the couple have been dating for three years.
The prince and Ms Middleton met during their first year at university, where they lived a few doors apart in halls of residence.
They later became housemates, sharing a four-bedroom Edwardian townhouse along with two friends, before moving a few miles to a cottage outside St Andrews.
Rumours of a romance intensified when they were later pictured on a skiing holiday in Klosters, Switzerland.
Last year, speculation of a royal engagement increased after Ms Middleton was the prince's guest at his passing-out parade at Sandhurst Military Academy in Camberley, Surrey, along with senior members of the royal family.
The 24 year-old prince is on duty with the Household Cavalry Blues and Royals and is due to begin a five-month training period in Dorset in March.
