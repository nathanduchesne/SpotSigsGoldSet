Saddam Hussein execution 'no later than tomorrow'


Publisher:  Ian Morgan
Published: 29/12/2006 - 18:53:23 PM Printable version
Send to a friend
 


Saddam Hussein
Relevant News

Saddam Hussein will be executed no later than Saturday, said an Iraqi judge authorised to attend his hanging, and American and Iraqi officials said the deposed president was still in the hands of American guards.

A senior Iraqi government official said a meeting would be held around 1900 GMT between officials from Prime Minister Nouri Maliki's office and US officials to set a time for the execution

The physical transfer of Saddam to Iraqi authorities is one of the last steps before his hanging.

"Saddam Hussein has not been turned over to Iraqi authorities," a US official said tonight.

Munir Haddad, a judge on the appeals court that upheld Saddam's death sentence, said he was ready to attend the execution.

"There is no reason for delays," Haddad said.

Maliki has signed Saddam's death sentence, the same senior Iraqi official said.

"We have agreed with the Americans that the handover will take place only a few minutes before he is executed," the official said.

Saddam's lawyers today issued a statement calling on "everybody to do everything to stop this unfair execution."
