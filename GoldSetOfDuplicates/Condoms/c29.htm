New York eyes own condom wrapper
Fri, January 26, 2007
By AP

NEW YORK -- Could this soon be available from city hall: an official New York condom in a jazzy wrapper, perhaps printed with a colourful subway map or some other city them?

New York City hands out 1.5 million free condoms a month in ordinary wrappers, but health officials figure people would be more likely to use them if the packaging were more distinctive.

"Brands work and people use branded items more than they use non-branded items, whether it's a cola or a medicine even," health commissioner Thomas Frieden said.

The health department, which uses the condom program to fight sexually transmitted diseases, said it still considering package designs.

The 18 million free condoms each year go to hundreds of organizations, which distribute them at health clinics, advocacy groups, bars, restaurants, nightclubs, nail salons and prisons.

City officials, who conduct an annual health survey, say a memorable package could also help them gauge how often people actually use the city's condoms.

"If they describe our wrapper, then we'll know that they would have used our condom," Frieden said.
