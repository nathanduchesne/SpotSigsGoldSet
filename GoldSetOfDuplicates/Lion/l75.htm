Hiker saves husband from lion

1/26/2007
SAN FRANCISCO (AP) - Wildlife officials on Thursday credited a woman with saving her husband's life by clubbing a mountain lion that attacked him while the couple were hiking in a California state park. Jim and Nell Hamm were hiking in Prairie Creek Redwoods State Park when the lion pounced.

"He didn't scream. It was a different, horrible plea for help, and I turned around, and by then the cat had wrestled Jim to the ground," Nell Hamm said. She said she grabbed a 4-inch-wide log and beat the animal with it, but it would not release its hold on her husband's head. "Jim was talking to me all through this, and he said, "I've got a pen in my pocket and get the pen and jab him in the eye,' " she said. "So I got the pen and tried to put it in his eye, but it didn't want to go in as easy as I thought it would."

When the pen bent, Nell Hamm went back to the log. The lion let go and, with blood on its snout, stood staring. She screamed, waving the log until it walked away. Jim Hamm is in fair condition and tests are being done to see if one of two lions killed after the attack was responsible.
