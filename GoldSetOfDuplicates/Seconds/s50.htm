Flight 5191 transcripts released

06:06 PM EST on Wednesday, January 17, 2007

The mystery of why a doomed Comair flight took off from wrong runway seemed to deepen Wednesday with the release of a transcript of comments by the pilots as the plane was taking off.

As the co-pilot guided the plane down the runway, he said, it was weird that the runway in Lexington had no lights.

Seconds before Comair Flight 5191 crashed near the Bluegrass Airport, the pilots apparently knew that something was wrong, but there was no time to react or prevent the crash.

The co-pilot, James Polhinke, who was flying the plane, said that it was "weird" that the runway had no lights.

The National Transportation Safety Bureau transcript, says the Captain Jeffrey Clay responded “yeah.” His one-word response was just seconds before impact.

The August 27 crash in the pre-dawn darkness killed 49 of the 50 people on board. The transcript says the last understandable word from the cockpit was Capt. Clay saying “whoa,” just a second before the crash.

Federal investigators say it was Clay who taxied the plane onto the wrong runway before Polhinke took over controls for the take-off.

Minutes before the crash, Polehinke said, “I'll take us to Atlanta,” and Clay responds, “Sure.”

Although he lost a leg and suffered brain damage, Polhinke was the lone survivor of the crash. Family members say he remembers nothing about the morning of the crash.

Although the plane clearly crashed because it took off on a runway that was too short, the NTSB has still not released a probable cause for the Comair crash.

Meanwhile, numerous lawsuits have been filed accusing Comair of negligence, but the airline also sued the Bluegrass Airport saying taxiways that were altered for construction were part of the cause.

From The Associated Press:

By JEFFREY McMURRAY / Associated Press Writer

WASHINGTON (AP) -- Seconds before the nation’s deadliest plane crash in 5 years, the co-pilot aboard Comair Flight 5191 noted it was “weird” that the runway at Lexington, Ky., had no lights, according to a transcript released Wednesday.

The Aug. 27 crash of the regional airliner killed 49 of 50 people on board. The ill-fated flight crashed in the pre-dawn darkness shortly after takeoff from the wrong runway, which was unlit and too short for a passenger jet.

According to the National Transportation Safety Board transcript, co-pilot James Polehinke said it was “weird with no lights,” just seconds before the sound of the impact was heard.  The captain, Jeffrey Clay, responded, “Yeah.”

Polehinke, the lone survivor, lost a leg and suffered brain damage from the crash. He has told family members he remembers nothing about that morning.
