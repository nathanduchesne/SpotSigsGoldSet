Comair Pilot: Lack Of Lights 'Weird' Before Crash

POSTED: 1:58 pm EST January 17, 2007

WASHINGTON -- Just seconds before the plane crashed in Kentucky, the co-pilot of a Comair flight remarked that it was "weird" that there were no lights on the runway.

The final seconds of that Aug. 27 flight are revealed in transcripts made public Wednesday by federal safety investigators.

The plane was trying to take off on the wrong runway in Lexington, Ky. The runway had no lights and was too short for a passenger jet.

After the co-pilot's comment, the captain responded "yeah" -- and soon after, said "whoa."

Then the sound of impact is heard, as Flight 5191 ended with the deaths of everyone but the co-pilot.

Forty-nine people died in the wreckage of America's deadliest plane crash in five years.

The co-pilot lost a leg, suffered brain damage, and said he doesn't remember a thing about what happened.
