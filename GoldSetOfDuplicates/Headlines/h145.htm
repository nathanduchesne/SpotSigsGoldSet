Queen Kate rumours abound
Wed, January 10, 2007
Banking on an imminent engagement, Prince William's girlfriend is being pestered by the British media.
By JILL LAWLESS, AP

LONDON -- She makes headlines for getting a parking ticket, newspapers comment on her fashion choices and her 25th birthday yesterday generated a media storm.

Prince William's girlfriend, Kate Middleton, may become Queen of England. Before then, she seems to be undergoing a similar ordeal to that endured by Lady Diana Spencer when she dated Prince Charles.

Pictures of the brunette fashion buyer fetch thousands of dollars from the tabloid press. Paparazzi follow her to work, on shopping trips and on nights out with the prince. One published snapshot showed Middleton receiving a parking ticket.

British bookmaking firm William Hill says it is so certain William will pop the question, it has stopped taking bets on whether the pair will get engaged; only on the date.

William Hill said it is a 4-5 probability the pair will announce their engagement in 2007 and is offering 3-1 odds they will marry this year.

The prince and Middleton have dated since they were both students at St. Andrews University in Scotland.

Born in the commuter town of Reading, west of London, Middleton, unlike most previous royal consorts, is middle-class.

Her father worked for an airline and her mother was a flight attendant. They now run a mail-order business specializing in children's parties.

As a high school student she attended an elite private school, where she played tennis and field hockey, before studying art history at St. Andrews. She and William shared a student house.

Middleton and the Royal Family want to avoid a repeat of the paparazzi hounding endured by William's mother, Diana, Princess of Wales, who was just 19 when she began dating Prince Charles.

It has been reported Middleton is to be given a round-the-clock guard from the police royal protection squad.
